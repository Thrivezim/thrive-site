Hormone Balance

🌸 Understanding Hormone Imbalance & Estrogen Dominance

Hormonal conditions like PCOS, endometriosis, fibroids, infertility, and hormonal acne are often linked to a state called estrogen dominance — where there’s too much estrogen relative to progesterone.

Estrogen is not the enemy — it's essential. But when it’s not properly metabolized or when detox pathways (like the liver) are overloaded, it can build up in the body and cause serious imbalances.

⚠️ Conditions Linked to Excess Estrogen:

  • PCOS: Often paired with insulin resistance and high estrogen, which disrupts ovulation and increases androgen levels
  • Endometriosis: Estrogen fuels the growth of endometrial tissue outside the uterus, worsening pain and inflammation
  • Fibroids: These estrogen-sensitive growths in the uterus thrive when estrogen is not balanced by progesterone
  • Infertility: Hormonal imbalances affect ovulation, egg quality, and uterine lining, making conception harder
  • Hormonal Acne: High estrogen or estrogen/testosterone imbalances can trigger excess sebum production and inflammation

🛑 What Drives Estrogen Dominance?

  • Poor liver detox (from alcohol, medication, sugar, poor diet)
  • Slow digestion and gut dysbiosis (constipation recycles estrogen)
  • Environmental estrogens (plastics, pesticides, fragrances, hormones in meat)
  • Insulin resistance and excess body fat (fat cells make more estrogen!)
  • High sugar and refined carb diets, which trigger insulin spikes and hormonal chaos

✅ How to Support Hormone Balance Naturally:

  • Support Liver Detox: Use herbs like Milk Thistle, eat cruciferous vegetables, avoid alcohol and processed foods
  • Improve Estrogen Metabolism: DIM helps convert estrogen into healthier metabolites; promote daily bowel movements
  • Balance Blood Sugar: Cut back on sugar and carbs; use berberine or apple cider vinegar; eat protein, fiber, and healthy fats
  • Reduce Toxin Exposure: Use natural personal care and cleaning products; store food in glass; eat organic
  • Lose Excess Body Fat (gently!): Fasting, walking, strength training, and carb control help
